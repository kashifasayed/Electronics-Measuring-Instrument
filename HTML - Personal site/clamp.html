Clamp Meter

  • A clamp meter is an electric test tool that combines a basic digital multimeter with a current sensor.

  • Clamps measure current. Probes measure voltage.

  • A current transformed inside the clamp meter senses the magnetic fluctuation and converts the value to an AC current reading. This type of measurement is convenient for measuring very high AC currents. Direct current, however, flows through conductors in a fixed polarity.

  • A clamp meter can check AC or DC voltage use to test continuity and also can check the flow of electric current through an electric circuit.

  • Can easily detect damage part, broken conductivity or excessive resistance in a circuit that is open circuit and discontinuity.

  • Clamp is around TWO wires nothing is measured, the current in the neutral wire cancels out the current in the phase/ live wire.

  • If the clamp is opened, value decreases even when the clamp is away from wire value decreases significant.

  • The measured value slightly influenced by the position of the wire inside the clamp.

    1)Current-sensing jaw.
    2)Tactile barrier (to protect fingers from shock).
    3)Hold button: Freezes the display readings. Reading is released when button is pushed a second time.

    5)Display.

    8)Inrush current button.
    9)Zero button (yellow):Removes DC offset from DC current.
    10)Jaw release lever.
    11)Alignment marks:To meet accuracy specification, a conductor must be aligned with these marks.
    12) Common input jack.
    13)Volts/ohm input jack.
    14)Input for flexible current probe.

    HOW DO CLAMP METER OPERATE?

    In general AC clamp meter operate on the principle of current transformation (CT) used to pick up magnetic flux generated
    as a result of current flowing through a conductor. Assuming a current flowing through a conductor to be the primary current,
    you can obtain a current proportional to the primary current by electromagnetic induction from the secondary winding of the
    transformer which is connected to a measuring circuit of the instrument. This permits you to take an AC current reading on the

    HOW DOES A CLAMP METER MEASURES DC/AC CURRENT?

  • Determine if the current to be measured is an AC or DC.

  • Select a clamp accessory for your multimeter that is designed to measure
    that specific current or one that can measure both AC and DC.

  • Determine if the range of your multimeter or clamp accessory is high enough
    to measure it. If not, select an instrument equipped for higher range.

  • To measure AC CURRENT with a current output clamp, turn the dial to mA/A.

  • Plug the black test lead into the COM jack.

  • Plug the red test lead into the mA/A jack.

  • To measure AC/DC current with a voltage output clamp,
    turn the dial to mVac for AC CURRENT or to mVdc for DC current.

  • Open the jaws by pressing the tools trigger.

  • Enclose a single conductor inside the jaws. Make certain the jaws are completely closed before taking readings.

    A clamp meter is primarily built for measuring current (or amperage), while a multimeter typically measures voltage, resistance, continuity, and sometimes low current. Nowadays, some clamps also include the measuring abilities of basic multimeters,
    and, likewise, some test meters allow for the attachment of an amp clamp (such as the Fluke iflex). Whereas clamp meters can measure high current, while multimeters have higher accuracy and better and better resolution.


    Clamp Meter

  • Lets you measure current without breaking the circuit.

  • Ideal for troubleshooting current in-situ.

  • Good for dual-purpose measuring,such as a machine speed as well as current drawn.

  • Generally lower accuracy and resolution.

  • Might struggle to open jaws in tightly-packed trunking.



    • Multi Meter

  • More measurement criteria, such as voltage, capacitance and frequency .

  • Measure electrical system to a higher resolution.

  • Better for electronics work.

  • Blows fuses on higher amps.

    • Therefore there used to be clamp meter vs multimeter difference, with a clamp primarily built to measure amperage
    and multimeter for voltage, resistance and establishing continuity, with less focus on current. Today,
    the line is blurred and a good model can be an electricians bosom buddy. Those requiring more accuracy
    would be better with a separate, high quality multimeter together with an amp clamp for heavier work.

    Benefits or Advantages of Clamp Meter


  • It can be used in noisy electrical environments.

  • It filters out noise and provides accurate and stable measurements such as motor starting current.

  • It has ergonomic design which fits in the hand while wearing protective equipment.

  • As mentioned it can be used in process and automation environment where it is not feasible to break the loop.

  • It can measure low level DC current.

  • It can be used for measurements in tight locations with clear presentation.

  • It is used for a wide range of measurements.

  • Due to fast measurements it saves both time and money.

    • Drawbacks or disadvantages of Clamp Meter


  • A clamp meter is a current measuring tool with some voltage measuring abilities.
